PEC: VERBAL VIOLENCE AND ADDITIONS
Verbal violence and addictions are two social problems that are often intertwined, affecting the mental, emotional and physical health of those who suffer from them. Although they appear to be different phenomena, both share the ability to generate a profound impact on people's lives and on the dynamics of interpersonal relationships. This essay aims to explore how verbal violence can contribute to the development of addictions and how addictions, in turn, can enhance verbal violence. Verbal violence and addictions are deeply interconnected problems that affect millions of people around the world. Both have the potential to cause significant harm to people's lives, and many times, one acts as a trigger for the other. Combating these issues requires a comprehensive approach that addresses both personal and social factors, promoting education, respect and emotional support for those caught in these destructive cycles. Only through a joint effort can the cycle of violence and addiction that affects so many people be broken, allowing the construction of healthier relationships and a more equitable and understanding social environment.
